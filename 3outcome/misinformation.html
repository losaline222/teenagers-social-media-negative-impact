What is Misinformation?

Misinformation refers to false or misleading information spread regardless of intent to deceive. It differs from disinformation, which is spread deliberately to mislead. Both types of false content can easily go viral on social media, amplifying their effects.

How Misinformation Spreads

  • Viral posts shared without fact-checking
  • Clickbait headlines that exaggerate or distort facts
  • False information from unreliable sources
  • Algorithm-driven content that prioritizes sensationalism

Consequences of Misinformation

Misinformation can have serious societal impacts, such as fueling polarization, influencing harmful behavior, eroding public trust, and distorting political and social discourse. In extreme cases, it can even result in violence and unrest.
