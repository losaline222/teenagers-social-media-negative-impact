Signs of Social Media Addiction

  • Constantly checking notifications and updates
  • Spending excessive amounts of time on social media
  • Neglecting real-world responsibilities
  • Feeling anxious or upset when unable to access social media
  • Relying on social media to feel connected or validated

Why Social Media is Addictive

Social media platforms are designed to capture attention and encourage users to spend more time on their sites. Features like notifications, likes, and the endless scroll exploit psychological triggers, such as dopamine release, which can make social media usage highly addictive.

Consequences of Addiction

Overuse of social media can result in several negative outcomes, including mental health issues like anxiety and depression, decreased productivity, and weakened interpersonal relationships. It can also lead to poor self-esteem due to constant comparison with others.
